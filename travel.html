Mexico City - July 2025

Starting Off Solo

Starting off in Mexico City...what an absolutely incredible time! I was the first in my group to land as I had mistakenly booked a flight that left at 2am whilst everyone else was leaving at 8am. From New York City the flight is about 5 hours, although the city is 2 hours behind EST. So as you can imagine, I landed at 5am Mexico City time and was quite tired. I had only realized I booked the wrong flight a few days before, so I solutioned until I came up with the bright idea to book one of the sleeping pods in the Mexico City airport. I was lucky that they offered these sleepiing pods at both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2. The two hour nap I took was an absolute game-changer as I hadn't slept much on the plane. The pod was literally a pod. Tiny, made of metal, and very minimalistic. Inside the pod they provided you with a sleeping mat, a blanket, and a small pillow. There was also a water bottle and outlet for charging as well as a mirror. The outlet in the first pod I booked wasn't working so I had them switch me to another pod. They also had bathroom facilities including showers.

Around 8am I left the sleeping pod and called my uber to my group's airbnb we had booked. Technically, we were not set to check-in until 12pm. This was a bit of a logistical issue for me and we messaged the airbnb host to ask if it would be alright if I came and dropped off my belongings early. We received no response unfortunately so when I called my uber to the neighborhood we were staying in, Condesa, I was going in blind. When I arrived, luckily I had the code from the check-in instructions and was able to successfully plug in the entry code into the keypad and walk in. When I stepped inside I heard the sound of vacuuming coming from upstairs and some women speaking in Spanish. I waited in the doorway deciding how best to approach these women without alarming them. I opted to wait until one of them came downstairs and used my conversational-level Spanish to mention that I had arrived early and would it be alright if I dropped off my bags in the house? Fortunately, the women were kind and understanding and were actually mostly done with the upstairs bedrooms.

I felt so free not having the literal weight of my massive backpack and carry-on on me. I still had 3 hours at this point until my friends arrived. I walked out of the airbnb, sleep deprived, a little delirious, but mostly excited for the trip ahead. I decided I would try and use the time I had alone logistically. I strolled through the tree lined streets of our new neighborhood (for 5 days) and looked up a nearby nail salon. I walked in and asked if they took walk in appointments. The receptionist spoke English but my nail-tech and I spoke Spanish the whole time. My conversational abilities had worked out for me so far but I was now struggling to remember the word to describe the nail shape and colors that I wanted. We eventually figured it out and I discovered that although much of the time I lacked specific words, there were always work-around solutions. After the nail salon I went to a nearby grocery store and got a few items I knew the group might need. I also texted everyone still on the plane to see if there were any requests. I got instant coffee, bananas, oat milk, takis, and strawberries. I walked back to the airbnb impressed with how much I could accomplish while being so sleep-deprived. After dropping off the groceries I went to a cafe at the end of our street and ordered a cold brew. I walked back to the house facetimed my dad to give him a virtual house tour while I waited for my friends to arrive. Mid-sentence showing my dad the upstairs balcony looking onto the floor below I heard the door open. My friends had arrived! Any tiredness I had felt that morning momentarily subsided and I was overcome with joy that they had all arrived safely and our trip could truly begin.
